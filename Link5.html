Interesting facts

🎬 1. The Wachowskis Had to Prove Themselves
Before the studio greenlit the full film, the Wachowskis
had to create a 10-minute proof-of-concept scene (the lobby shootout)
to convince Warner Bros. that they could pull off such a complex movie.

🥋 2. Actors Trained for Months
The main cast (Keanu Reeves, Carrie-Anne Moss, Laurence Fishburne)
trained in martial arts for 4 months, 6–8 hours a day, with choreographer
Yuen Woo-ping, one of the top action coordinators in Hong Kong cinema.

🕶️ 3. Iconic Sunglasses
Each character's sunglasses were custom-designed to reflect their personality.
For example, Morpheus’s lenses are round and philosophical-looking, while Neo’s are sleek and minimal.

💊 4. Red Pill vs. Blue Pill
The "red pill vs. blue pill" choice became a pop culture symbol.
It’s now used in debates about truth, reality, and awakening to uncomfortable knowledge.

🧠 5. The Code Is Not What You Think
The green falling Matrix code is actually made up of Japanese
katakana characters, numbers, and random symbols. The designer said it was inspired by sushi recipes from his wife's Japanese cookbooks!

🕴️ 6. Keanu Had Neck Surgery
Keanu Reeves had neck surgery before training began and wore a
neck brace during the early months of martial arts prep. Some
scenes were adapted to be less physically intense for him.

💥 7. Bullet Time Was Revolutionary
The "bullet time" effect, where time slows and the camera spins
around the action, was a groundbreaking technique at the time.
It combined CGI with dozens of still cameras in a circular setup.

🔄 8. The Matrix Is Full of Philosophy
The film is packed with references to Buddhism, Christianity, Plato’s
Allegory of the Cave, and French postmodern philosophy—especially

questions about reality and perception.
🎭 9. Will Smith Turned Down Neo
Will Smith was originally offered the role of Neo but turned it down to do
Wild Wild West. He later admitted he didn’t understand the script at the time.

🖥️ 10. The Matrix Inspired Countless Other Films
The Matrix had a massive impact on action and sci-fi cinema. Its visual style,
slow-motion action, and philosophical depth have influenced everything from Inception to John Wick.
