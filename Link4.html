Difficulties

The creation of The Matrix faced several difficulties,
including the development of innovative visual effects like the famous
bullet time, which required the design of new techniques with dozens of
cameras and was expensive and complex to execute. Furthermore, the script
was too philosophical and confusing for the studios, so the directors struggled
to convince Warner Bros. to finance the project, to the point that they had to
reshoot the opening scene of Trinity to demonstrate the film's potential.
There were also great physical demands, as the actors had to train for months
in martial arts despite injuries and limitationsâ€”such as Keanu Reeves,
who was recovering from neck surgery, and Carrie-Anne Moss, who hid a sprained
ankle to avoid losing the role. Added to all this was a tight initial budget
and distrust from the producers, but the effort ultimately
resulted in a work that revolutionized science fiction and action films.
